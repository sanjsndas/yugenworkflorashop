Rooted in Yūgen: Beauty & Impermanence

Where the depth of green meets the vitality of red — a sanctuary for mindful gardeners and nature lovers.

The Yugen Flora Story

Founded in 2018 by botanist and designer Mikaela Rojas, Yugen Flora Workshop emerged from a deep longing to blend Japanese aesthetic principles — the mystery and grace of “Yūgen” — with the bold energy of red botanical accents. We believe gardens are living poetry: every leaf whispers patience, every crimson petal speaks of passion.

Our Portland-based studio and garden sanctuary has grown into a collective of horticulturists, artists, and eco-educators. We design immersive green spaces that celebrate contrast: deep emerald ferns against fiery Japanese maples, red salvias dancing among silver-green artemisia.

Our Guiding Colors

Green · 緑

Growth, resilience, and harmony. Green embodies the earth’s quiet wisdom — from healing herbs to shade-giving canopies. We cultivate sustainable practices, native plants, and soil regeneration.

Red · 赤

Energy, courage, and transformation. Red is the heartbeat of the garden: blooming camellias, ripe berries, and dramatic foliage. It reminds us to celebrate bold moments in nature's cycle.

Mindful Cultivation

We teach slow gardening, seasonal observation, and the art of pruning as meditation. Every workshop encourages you to listen — to the soil, the weather, and your own rhythm.

The Seed of Inspiration

Yugen Flora Workshop began as a small urban plot behind a reclaimed warehouse. Surrounded by concrete, Mikaela planted her first crimson maple and a grove of shade-loving hostas. What started as a personal retreat soon grew into a community hub for everyone craving a connection with living plants.

Today, we operate a 1.5-acre educational garden featuring over 200 species of green and red plants — from rare red hellebores to lush moss gardens. We host seasonal festivals, ikebana intensives, and organic gardening courses. Our flagship ‘Crimson & Canopy’ design philosophy has been featured in Pacific Horticulture and Gardenista.

We are proud to be a woman-led, BIPOC-friendly space that champions regenerative landscaping and urban biodiversity.

Green & Red Manifesto

“We don’t just plant gardens — we cultivate relationships. Between chlorophyll and carotene, between calm and courage. In every workshop, we ask: How does this green root anchor you? How does this red bloom ignite you?”

Our Promise: Every design, seed, and lesson is guided by ecological integrity, artistic wonder, and the joy of working with our hands. We use organic fertilizers, avoid synthetic pesticides, and champion climate-appropriate plants. The red thread that runs through all our work: inclusivity, beauty, and resilience.
